Synopsis

One morning at about 5AM, a highly committed, educated, and talented person sits in the dark on a tennis court. Day after day he plays tennis alone in the dark and later in the morning light with others. Day after day he makes a fair amount of money as a software expert, and tries to unwind by 8PM so he can go to sleep. He is basically a border collie: ever eager to work and to please.

This particular morning he has just returned from the location of a major tennis tournament, sixteen hours round-trip away. Winning this tournament would be financially lucrative and would shock the world given he is forty eight years old. Losing the tournament is certainly likely, but failing doesn’t phase him. The winning part really phases him. “Why would I want to make money that way?”

As his subconscious and conscious brain navigate through abilities and possibilities, this older, balding, white male in great intellectual and physical shape realizes only one job is perfect for him and he may be perfect for that job. That more than four decades of incredible focus and progress has turned him into the perfect executor. Or delivery boy. You tell him what you want, and he delivers it to you. The perfect executor is somewhat insane by most people’s beliefs about the world. But this is something he is used to.

And so a few minutes later he has his next career focus. He wants to be great at one job, and to try to get that one job. President. President of the United States of America. Not just any president, an Insanely Great President. A president better for the American people and even all of humanity than has been seen in decades.

This book will describe the preparation and the candidacy of a very different kind of person for one of the most important jobs on the planet. Whether I get elected or not, it will show people a different kind of president, and how that Insanely Great President would be better at serving them than anyone they have ever elected before.
