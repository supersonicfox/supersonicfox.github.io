What Does the Fox Wear? (2)

Dec 27th, 2014

Tennis is a major part of my life, but it can not interfere with the “normal” part of my life:

  • I must make money
  • I must spend time with my family
  • I must sleep
  • I must do a bunch of other crazy things, like write books, teach kids, etc.

How I manage this is that tennis normally ends by about 8AM, or sometimes 9:30AM on Saturdays. But I have to put in about 20-30 hours a week to keep my game at the level it is. That is at least three hours a day, so I have to start about 5AM every day.

There are some flaws with starting at 5AM. First, it is quite dark. The sun is rising at almost 7AM now, so that is two hours of no sun. At 6AM I can turn on the lights, so I work out for about an hour without much light. A street lamp lights part of the court, and your eyes adjust to the dark.

The second flaw is that although I can turn on lights, I can not turn on the heat lamps. Because there aren’t any heat lamps to turn on. California is pretty temperate by the coast line, so this isn’t a big deal most of the year, but today was ‘notable’:

There is no bad weather

I believe in the maxim “There is no bad weather, just bad clothing”, so when I see those numbers on my dashboard I do the obvious thing: “Winter Up!”. Seeing freezing temperatures when thinking ‘Tennis’ does not illicit particularly useful images. But thinking ‘Winter Sport’ conjures images of downhill and cross-country skiers, sled-teams, and outdoor speed skaters. These people can handle the weather, so why can’t I?
