End of Season

The last paid match I competed in was Moraga, with a $25K pot. They had very weird courts, and I lost to a grinder when I stupidly tried to grind back. The cause was partially the courts (very slow), and partially watching someone else who plays similar to me being beaten. Both of these reasons are completely idiotic reasons to not play your game. Just because someone else is losing with your style doesn’t mean you will lose. And if you have a pressure-based game, you need to boost the pressure on slow courts to make up for the slow courts. Very stupid and disappointing, but given it was the last tournament of the year (of note), that meant I had a lot of time to prepare for the next one. Indian Wells, 2016. Six months away.

In 2015 I played in six or so tournaments. That is not a lot, but it gave me a lot of information. I know what I won’t be (six foot six), what I don’t want (grind-away), and what I may not want (Rafa’s forehand… very dangerous physiologically). I have a lot of assets: wicked backhand, wicked return, broad collection of serves, and psychological stability. I have liabilities like I don’t cheat on line calls.

So the question is “where to go” for 2016? The answer is the same as it has always been “I want to play like Roger Federer”. To an amateur, I looked a bit like that in 2015. But my coach knew otherwise and, given my progress, has finally shown me some of the final (maybe) secrets to Roger’s strokes. I can’t really go over how they work, because they are very dangerous to your body if done wrong. But the simple summary is they make the racket work very much like Indiana Jones’ bull whip. It is not just one technique, but a collection of pieces working together that makes for a very powerful, efficient, and compressed, stroke. It enables you to focus on “get to the ball” and “place the ball” without the stroke being of real concern.

There are a bunch of other things too, and there are several months to go.

Off Season

The season is over, they mornings are much darker, and winter rains are coming in. Off season for outdoor tennis is not very pleasant. But tennis is one of my professions, and I do enjoy it. It is meditative, social, and physically beneficial. Still, In The Rain looms :–/
