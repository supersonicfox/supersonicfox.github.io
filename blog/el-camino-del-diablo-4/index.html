El Camino Del Diablo (4)

Nov 19th, 2014

Continuing the road to the devil…

Serve ‘F’

Serve ‘E’ does not make a lot of sense as an actual serve, but it is highly useful in a game. If you hit a ball to me at about should height, you will get a snap serve back. Basically serve ‘E’ but with a snap (to be explained later). Actually, at this point, if you hit a ball to me above my waist-line you may get a snap serve back. You probably will not like that… but I digress.

Serve ‘F’ is a pure slice. Again, you must leverage the fact that you are a box and make sure the ball is in the back of the box and generally a bit higher than your head. Then you slice it just like you did with ‘E’. Very nice slice. You must put everything you can into slicing that ball to really see how wonderful a serve this is. And after you have done all of that, I will show you more.

Serve ‘G’

Serve ‘G’ is among the most fun serves to do as well as the most useless in an actual game. To do a ‘G’ serve, your racket is parallel to the ground. Yes, facing down. If you get better it is facing down and backward.

The goal of the ‘G’ serve is to produce maximum spin with minimal ball travel. A perfect ‘G’ does not travel forward at all. The ball simply rises up in the air, hits the ground, and goes backward from where you are standing. You are slicing the air with your racket, and the ball just happens to be there to feel the slice. If another person was there… well… they wouldn’t be very happy.

As with all serves, you should be able to put everything you have into this serve. Yesterday I was measured to produce about 800 watts per arm, and I put 800 watts into Serve ‘G’. ‘G’ is awesome. And useless.
