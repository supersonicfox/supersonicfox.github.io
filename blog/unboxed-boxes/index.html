Unboxed Boxes

Oct 25th, 2014

I do not want you to be ‘Boxed’, to be constrained by artificial constraints or limitations you believe exist but have never verified or actively chosen. I hate that kind of boxing and a huge part of SOPA http://SlumsOfPaloAlto.com/ is getting people to recognize the boxing that occurs and get rid of it in themselves and others.

On the other hand, you must recognize that you are a box… at least pretty close to a box… physically.

Quadrants around Boxes

Previously I described the quadrants around your body, but in that I treated you as if you were a point in space. But looking down at you, your left and right shoulders are clearly visible as different things. Assuming you swing your arms vertically in a circle around your shoulders, you form a box: your right swinging arm on the right of you, and your left swinging on the left of you.

To get to your ultimate game, you have to let the ball (or position yourself to enable the ball to) get to racket-side of this box.

Volleys

For example, a backhand volley is innately easier than a forehand volley because you have both: “Get to the ball soon” combined with “Hit it on the racket-side of the box”. You rotate your body for a backhand and your racket is automatically close to the net. Actually pretty much everything is easier for your backhand, because of this ‘box’ rotation. The only thing that is harder are the strokes that rely on lots of rotation, because you inherently don’t need to, so don’t practice, rotational backhands.

People actually comment on my backhand more than almost anything else about my game. I do like my one-handed-bullet-and-pretty-much-whatever-I-want backhand, but it isn’t my best shot. It just isn’t “weak”: I hit it as well as a backhand can be hit. But most people fear their backhands and so they avoid and don’t practice it. My model is simply “Hit my weakest shot” and over time that rotates around and all my shots are “my weakest shot”… that makes you very dangerous when your weakest shot is the shot that everyone complements you on.

Forehand Slice

The box is the most frustrating when dealing with forehand slices, including forehand volleys. You want to hit the ball… soon… right now!… but no… you must wait for it to enter your box and almost leave it out the right/rear side of the box. Slices hit late can actually be fine with the right grip, but power slices hit early in the box just fly off the court :–( Very frustrating and likely to make you want to hit even earlier which is exactly the wrong reaction. Be patient!
