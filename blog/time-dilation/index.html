Time Dilation

Dec 11th, 2014

I stand near the service line (halfway) when I return serve. People find this unimaginable, even inconceivable. The ball is coming at 120 MPH and you are standing there?!?!

The answer to how I can stand there has two parts, but the first is basically ‘Time Dilation’. Just like computers do a lot of things in a short amount of time, you too can do a lot of things in a short amount of time. You just don’t realize it. You are operating on ‘slow’.

The difference between ‘slow’ and ‘fast’ is not the effort, energy, or speed aspects of what a person does. It is changing the unit of time at which the person does exactly the same things you are doing on ‘slow’. So the simplest way to think about this is to adjust time to be in equivalent ‘slow’ units vs. thinking the person is doing more in a unit of time. This is exactly how 120 FPS becomes 30 FPS.

For most people, thinking in ‘tens’ is the easiest. So imagine I (or maybe Roger) is operating at 10x the speed of you on the tennis court. Where one second passes for you, ten seconds passed for Roger. Where three tenths of a second passed for you, three seconds passed for Roger. You can get a lot done in three seconds! And have a sip of coffee to boot.

If you want to see the time dilation in action, just slow down the video of Roger to going at 1/10th its normal speed. I believe you will see continuous motion at that speed. Or something really close to it. Now do the same of some video of your club matches. And everyone will look ‘frozen’. That is time dilation. Mages are in continuous motion at about 10x the speed of muggles.
