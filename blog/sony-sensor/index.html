Sony Sensor

May 17th, 2015

I am technologically skilled and believe that some technologies can actually make us more effective people. But it isn’t a guarantee, on either the technology’s part or the persons part. I worked for a heath and wellness company to try to make exercise more fun, I had a “running” GPS-based tracker for my skating routine as well as a heart-rate monitor, and I have an unusual number of GoPros. At least for me, all of these things are fun for a while and then the novelty and utility wear off. That seems to be a common pattern.

For tennis: a racket, a few balls, and focused practice are far better than any technology I have tried. Possibly Goliath (a http://www.sportsattack.com/store/ace-attack-tennis-machine/) is an exception if you can’t find a partner that good, but the overhead of Goliath is quite high. In any case, despite the ephemeral relationship, I continue to try new technology. And the newest of the new is a very nicely designed monitor that happens to work with my racket.

Sony finally released its monitor to the states: http://www.smarttennissensor.sony.net/NA/ and as of a few days ago, I turned mine on. It is a nice looking device: simple and compact. It doesn’t weigh very much, and that type of weight on the butt of your racket can actually help with certain advanced strokes. As of this writing, I have played with it for several days. Not all of it’s features, but definitely working through it’s core capability. But in reality, it revealed it’s good and bad in the first ten minutes.
