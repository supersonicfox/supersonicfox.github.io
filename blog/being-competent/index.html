Being Competent

Nov 21st, 2014

Continuing my ‘insanity’ subtopic, or slightly related to it, I am now going to properly define ‘competent’, and explain how you become competent at something, and how long this takes. The answer may surprise you because it makes complete sense, but is not how people normal commonly (incorrectly) define the term.

An important realization is that ‘Competent’ is completely orthogonal to (independent of, think North-South vs. East-West) ‘Skill’. I am a skilled and competent tennis player. I may be a 5.5. Not totally sure. Will figure that out at the next tournament. With competitive sports it is very hard to become incompetent If you beat Roger, you are #1. If you lose to him, you probably are not. If you can’t even see his serve, you are likely a lot less skilled than Roger. Almost all sports have this wonderful characteristic: on game day every one is competent… or simply in denial. No incompetency at all.

I am also a highly unskilled and competent brain surgeon. I will not operate on your brain. Period. Just not going to happen. If we are trapped in the woods and I have to chop off you leg to get you out of a bear trap… I will first warn you that I am similarly unskilled as a leg-chopper-offer. I am competent at it, but you are likely to die if you ask me to do it.

With the second example, you can see that being competent takes absolutely no time! At all. None. Phew! Thought it was hard.

Ah…. but it can be hard. It gets hard to become competent when you are trapped in being incompetent. To be incompetent probably takes some amount of time… you invested a year or two in improving your tennis game… and during that time you thought your were getting better and better… you were sure it was the case. An hour here and there… adds up to 100 hours… over five years, maybe 500 hours… You put in all time, so you must be ‘Good’.

But that is delusional. You aren’t good because your put in time. One becomes good by progressing through failure. Over… and over… and over… Playing 500 hours of club tennis makes you as skilled in tennis as… have no idea. But you can figure it out. Find a 4.0 and play them. Oh… they might be incompetent too and are really a 3.0? Yes. That is the said part. If you get enough incompetent people together, you have no clue.

That is why I am going to a spin-clinic tomorrow, why I have nine go-pros, and why I am going to a true “Kill each other” kind of Open in the spring. Because I know I am not incompetent… but I have no idea what my skill level really is. I don’t care very much, but I am curious.

Never going to be even a moderately skilled brain surgeon. Never is enough for that one.
