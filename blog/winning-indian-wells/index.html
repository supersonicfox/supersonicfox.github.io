Winning Indian Wells

Jan 10th, 2015

Today I registered for Indian Wells ATP challenger tournament. If I make it through the challenger, I go through a qualifier tournament. If I make it through the qualifier, I get into the main tournament, where Roger and Rafa will be “waiting”. If I beat all of my side of the ladder, I will have either beaten Roger and Rafa, or will have beaten the people that beat these Tennis giants. I will also make quite a bit of money and likely make a bit of news.

I expect to win it all, so this year may be a pretty notable year in my life.

If you have read my writings here and there, you may realize I am not a normal person. My expectations are lofty, my abilities bizarre, and my life either a dreamy heaven or a living hell depending on how you look at it. A lot of me is not transferable. You can’t just be me. But this one aspect of me, you can take to yourself like the transfer of the Olympic flame. You should expect great things. From yourself. And then do your best to make those great things happen.

I am not sure how those words ring on your ears: “You should expect great things. From yourself. And then do your best to make those great things happen.” My guess is that you just kept reading along. But I didn’t write those three sentences for you to read them and move along. They are a torch. They are fire. You should read and repeat them. Aloud. Like a Koan. I will even change the pronoun and structure to make that easier: “I expect {something great} from myself. And I am now doing {all the things that requires} to make that happen”.

This phrase is far more powerful than a New Years resolution. Because although it could be lofty in its expectation, it is grounded in its action. “I expect to win Indian Wells, and beat Roger and Rafa. So I am now playing tennis forty hours a week in weather similar to Indian Wells”. I am prepared to not win Indian Wells, but I expect to win or why am I going down there? I am going to do everything now that I could possibly do to prepare now to win now, or again, why am I going down there? If I am under-prepared… then I need to get prepared… or i shouldn’t go into the jungle.

This one thought is the difference between professionals and the rest: a true professional expects to win now, to do now, and is preparing now for what their profession requires. A professional is a soldier. Without the downside.
