El Camino Del Diablo (5)

Nov 22nd, 2014

Continuing the road to the devil…

Serve ‘H’

Serve ‘H’ is the serve this series is actually named after. It is the ‘Hell’ serve. It is my first “this is only a jump serve”, but to understand that you need to watch ‘Roger Rabbit’. So go watch that… I will wait…

Flow

The key to all tennis and martial arts motions is ‘flow’. You flow your body through the motions that you want your body to do. But it isn’t just any-old-flow. It is a “get real fast” and “woah horses” pair. It turns out that the “woah horses” part is the harder part. Because you don’t really want to halt your horses, you want them to just relax. To slow down on their own. Stop being ‘fast’ and somehow you will get back to some reasonable velocity. And by horses… I really mean ‘Joints and Muscles’ because I am pretty sure you don’t have horses inside your body. Even if you are a Unicorn yourself.

I can’t teach you here how to become slow. And slow down. Please seek John and Steve and my other mages if you want to learn that. But be aware that slowing down is critical to the road. If you can slow down, basically without brakes, then you are ready to speed up. Don’t continue until then.

Coil, Spring

All real tennis serves are jump serves. You might not see it visibly, but a jump serve is simply a “Coil, Spring” serve, which is the ultimate version of “flow” ignoring the slow down part I addressed above.

To serve a real serve, you toss the ball, and then immediately go get it. Coil, Toss, Spring. I tend to say the “Coil” to myself in a nice low voice. Not sure what actor I am channeling for the Coil. But for the ‘Spring’ I channel Roger Rabbit (you did see the movie right? If not, please go back to paragraph-1).

So “Cooooiiiiilllll” , “SPRING!”. “Cooooiiiillll”, “SPRING!”.

I actually hit some spectacularly bad serves when I start off, because I am not really ready to “SPRING!”. But all my serves have this rhythm. I jump-serve all my serves. The only thing that varies is where the ball is.

Serve ‘A’ prime

The value of Serve ‘A’ (my flat butterfly serve) is that it is simple. It has a nice continuous flow, so slowing down is not a problem. That makes it great for testing new things out. A-prime is a test of “Toss that ball somewhere crazy” and “Go get it”.

If you have a jump serve, the coil puts your body into a jump-capable position. So, if you toss the ball somewhere ‘stupid’ you should be able to jump after it. And get to it. If you can get all this timing right.

Again, I can’t help you with all the timing, physical fitness, etc. Blogs and books aren’t a good medium, and I haven’t taken enough time to process all my videos.

But the basic concept is: “Toss the ball forward about one foot, and then do ‘Serve A’ given the ball is there”.

Doing this with your ‘A’ serve likely produces a little more speed on the ball and reduces the amount of time people have to react to the ball. You are closer to them. You hit it faster. Very obnoxious.

But this is basically the same serve as an ‘A’, so I don’t label it differently. It is just a more serious ‘A’

Serve ‘H’

The Hell serve is a different serve from all that preceeded it. It is actually a jumped ‘C’ (topspin) serve, but unlike a jumped ‘A’ this produces something radically different. Because you tossed the ball so ‘wrong’ for a ‘C’, when you actually hit the ball, it similarly comes out ‘wrong’ and is ridiculously flat. A proper ‘C’ is a nice loopy serve. A proper ‘H’ is flat.

Until it hits the ground. When all hell breaks loose. A proper ‘H’ has all the top-spin of a ‘C’ but that is concealed in an ‘A’ like trajectory. When an ‘H’ hits ground, it transforms into a ‘C’ and worse, and that ball kicks all over the place.

When I learned ‘H’ I thought I had been given a gift of the nastiest serve on the planet.

But the letters continue…
