Quadrants

Oct 22nd, 2014

Quadrants

Serving can be simplified into ‘toss ball’, ‘hit ball’. You can have one serve or a dozen, but all of them have this core rhythm. If you do have a dozen serves, they do not “all work” with every kind of toss. I have an underhand slice serve that starts with me dropping the ball right in front of me… very hard to hit top-spin on that one. I would even say ‘Inconceivable’.

To understand the toss, it can be helpful to think of yourself in the middle of the universe. Where is your body? And relative to that body, where is that ball?

Assuming you have only overhead serves, then the ball better be ‘up’. But “up where?”.

Four Quadrants

In plane geometry there are four quadrants going from I (1) to IV (4): http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quadrant_(plane_geometry). If you consider your body as the center of the universe, and you are right-handed, your relative quadrants match the geometry ones exactly. If you are left-handed, please flip the even/odd ones.

So there are four quadrants around your body, and the ball is always in one of them (or on a line, but let’s not be picky about that). So where is it? And where should it be? The obvious answer is “It depends”.

Different serves, different quadrants

For my butterfly, flat, ‘A’ serve, I contact in ‘I’. My body rotates so my right shoulder is forward (parallel to the baseline plus or minus) before contact, which basically guarantees I have to hit in ‘I’. The ballistic serve ‘B’ is behind me, so in either ‘IV’ or maybe ‘III’. And ‘C’ is a top-spin serve, so you want to be mostly going ‘up’ and that means the ball is in ‘III’.

Note that the quadrants exist all the time and if your body is moving, the quadrants are changing because of your movement. Later I will explain my ‘hell’ serve, which is basically a serve that you toss deep into ‘I’ and then get your body in the right spot to hit top spin on it: i.e. ‘III’.

Proper ball position

The quadrants are a great way to start being aware of ‘proper ball position’. There are others too, but lets stop with this for the moment.
