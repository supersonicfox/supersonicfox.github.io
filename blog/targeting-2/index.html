Targeting (2)

Dec 25th, 2014

Previously I discussed targeting rackets as part of serving (http://supersonicfox.com/blog/targeting-rackets/). The critical part of targeting rackets is to realize you are targeting above the net, not the court itself. And it also makes sure you always have a good, repeatable, frame of reference.

The other day it dawned on me that I use this for all shots, and that other people may not. I don’t hit into the net very much. I hit the top of the net, and I hit long, but into the actual net? Not likely after I have warmed up. How can that be? Why do I rarely hit into the net, where others do quite often? Well, I believe it turns out that it is related to targeting again. If you look at the following picture:

You will see four “Court Targets” identified. I am standing in the center of the court on the baseline, and say I drop a ball to my right-hand forehand or left-hand backhand. Where am I to target (mentally) to hit ‘CT1’? What path should the ball take?

Unfortunately, I think a lot of people think this is right:

That is unfortunately hopelessly wrong. Even if the ball shot out of my eye, it is still below the top of the net. And the ball isn’t going to shoot out of my eye. A normal drop and the ball is about waist high, and about two feet to my right.

The right picture is this:

By targeting the ‘yellow’ I have now gotten the ball over the net, and by ‘considering’ the green, I have now dealt with the parallax issue between my eye and the rightness of the ball compared to my eye. My eye targets yellow, but my hit stroke targets green. And the green line should be the trajectory of the ball. Makes sense? If so, you may now be able to shoot a bow better too :–)

But wait! Why the bottom of the windscreen? That is so high, and the ball will be way over the net. Well, if you ever played with me, you will notice my balls are barely over the net. Like millimeters. And that is basically because targeting the bottom of the windscreen is targeting directly over the net. The second parallax effect is the difference between your eye and the ball vertically. A picture from ball height should make that second perspective crystal clear :–)
