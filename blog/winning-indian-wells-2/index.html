Winning Indian Wells (2)

Feb 1st, 2015

One of the biggest issues I have with winning Indian Wells is that I will be playing against people that are significantly better than people I play against now. Tennis, like almost all sports, does not involve your conscious brain during play. Or very little of it. Tennis play is dominated by sub-second reactions. With balls traveling at 120 MPH, they are traveling at 2 MPM or 10000 FPM, and finally about 160 FPS. Although not this regular, you can think of a tennis court as being divided into four 20-foot sections from end to end. So baseline to baseline is a half a second. Net to baseline is a quarter of a second. Baseline to service is three eights of a second. Yes, the times are a little longer as speeds go down, but no matter what you are sub-second at the professional levels except for defensive lobs.

A half a second is not a useful amount of time to think about something. You must be leveraging your reactions to something similar that you have seen before and let your body take over.

When I did a tournament at Stockton, I encountered something I hadn’t seen for more than thirty years. A good left-handed player. His shots weren’t faster than anything I had seen before. His serve wasn’t stronger. But everything was “off” and I couldn’t get my game together against him. There are a number of causes for this, but certainly one was that his spins were just ‘different’ and I was not subconsciously used to them.

So to get ready for Indian Wells, John was nice enough to serve left-handed to me. And I have an insanely great ball machine that can serve left handed too. With exactly four weeks to go, we will see if I can level up by day-1.
