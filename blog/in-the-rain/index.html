In the Rain

Nov 30th, 2014

It is raining in California. This is a good thing, because we need a lot of water and we don’t have it. The rain causes my internet to go out (another story), which is actually enjoyable because the internet is a dangerous drug. Cold turkey the drug!

It doesn’t rain very much in California any more: climate change has caused our weather to be ‘nicer and nicer’. Tennis is played outside, so tennis benefits from this niceness. It is hard to not be able to play tennis in California (or at least the Bay Area).

You can certainly play tennis in the rain. Don’t play if you start sliding around, but other than that, there is absolutely nothing preventing playing in the rain. Yes, the balls will get wet and be no longer Wimbledon quality. Yes, the racket will get wet and you may have to replace the grip or even the strings. So what?

Well, for most people, even a drizzle makes them go somewhere else. On my courts, when I manage to get two-three hours in a day (in the rain or between spells), almost no one else shows up at all.

Better courts after a rain

If it has recently rained but is no longer raining, you can get your courts (hard courts) in better shape by leveraging the power of the sun. The sun is really good at drying things, but it needs ‘to see’ the water. Puddles are not good (can see the top but not below), so you should squeegee puddles and spread them around. You can also blow things around with an electric blower, mop with a blanket, push with a broom, etc.

If you get the water spread out, it takes about 15-30 minutes for a wet court to be quite playable. So you can choose your ball-wetness to wait time.

I rarely wait for much of anything nowadays :–)
